Housing Search Timeline

Start your housing search at the right time to get the best options and avoid stress.

January - February (Peak Season) ‚≠ê

This is the most competitive time for student housing. Landlords list properties for the upcoming academic year, and the best options go quickly. Start searching early!

  • Set up saved searches on HomeMore and enable notifications
  • Contact landlords within 24 hours of new listings
  • Be prepared to tour properties within 2-3 days
  • Have your roommates decided and ready to commit
  • Prepare necessary documents (ID, references) in advance

March - April (Still Good Options)

Many quality properties are still available, but the best deals may be taken. You'll need to act decisively and be slightly more flexible.

  • Expand your search criteria slightly (distance, price range)
  • Consider properties just outside your initial preferences
  • Be ready to make decisions quickly after viewings
  • Negotiate lease terms - landlords may be more flexible now
  • Look for properties with multiple bedrooms if you're flexible on roommates

May - August (Limited but Possible)

Options are limited, but you might find deals. Some students drop out creating last-minute openings, and landlords may reduce prices to fill vacancies.

  • Check for sublease opportunities from students studying abroad
  • Be very flexible with move-in dates and lease terms
  • Monitor listings daily - openings appear suddenly
  • Consider less popular locations that may have availability
  • Reach out directly to property management companies
‚ö†Ô∏è Pro Tip: Don't wait until summer! The majority of quality off-campus housing is secured by March. Starting your search in January gives you the best selection and leverage for negotiations.

Understanding Your Budget

Create a realistic budget that includes ALL housing-related expenses, not just rent.

Monthly Rent

Typical Williamsburg Range: $600-$1,200 per person depending on location, amenities, and property condition.

  • Closer to campus = higher rent, but lower transportation costs
  • Newer/renovated properties typically cost $200-400 more per month
  • Ask if utilities are included (this can save $100-200/month)
  • Factor in whether parking is included or costs extra
  • Ask about annual rent increases for multi-year leases

Utilities (Often Not Included)

Budget: $100-$200 per month for electricity, water, gas, internet, and trash collection.

  • Ask current or previous tenants about average utility bills
  • Electric bills are higher in summer (AC) and winter (heating)
  • Check if internet is included or if you need to set it up
  • Understand how utilities are split among roommates
  • Some landlords cap utilities - know what happens if you exceed

Security Deposit & Upfront Costs

Typical Amount: Equal to one month's rent (e.g., if rent is $900, deposit is $900). You get this back if you leave the property in good condition.

  • Document property condition thoroughly at move-in with photos
  • Note any existing damage to avoid being charged later
  • Understand the deposit return timeline (usually 30-45 days after move-out)
  • Ask what conditions result in deposit deductions
  • Some places require first month + last month + deposit upfront

Additional Monthly Costs

These expenses add up quickly and are often forgotten in initial budgets.

  • Renter's Insurance: $15-30/month (often required, protects your belongings)
  • Groceries: $200-400/month (you're not on a meal plan anymore!)
  • Transportation: Gas, car maintenance, or WATA bus passes
  • Household Supplies: Cleaning products, toilet paper, paper towels
  • Parking Permits: Some complexes charge $25-50/month

One-Time Setup Costs

Budget $500-1,500 for initial setup depending on what you already own.

  • Application Fee: $25-50 per person
  • Furniture: Couch, dining table, desk, bed frame if not included
  • Kitchen Essentials: Dishes, pots, pans, utensils
  • Cleaning Supplies: Vacuum, mop, broom, cleaning products
  • Utility Setup Fees: Some utilities charge activation fees
üí° Budget Calculator: Total Monthly Cost = Rent + Utilities ($100-200) + Insurance ($20) + Groceries ($300) + Transportation ($50-100) = Typically $1,070-$1,520 per month. Make sure you can comfortably afford this!

How to Search Effectively

Maximize your search efforts with these proven strategies from students who've successfully found great housing.

Use Multiple Platforms

Don't rely on just one source. Many Williamsburg landlords only post on certain platforms, so you need comprehensive coverage.

  • HomeMore: Start here for W&M-specific listings with verified reviews
  • Apartments.com: Aggregates listings around Williamsburg, often showing pricing and amenities
  • Facebook Groups & Student Boards: Roomshares, subleases, and last-minute openings
  • Set up alerts to be notified immediately of new listings

Set Filters and Priorities

  • Decide your maximum rent early
  • Determine must-have amenities (furnished, parking, utilities included)
  • Decide proximity to campus vs. cost tradeoff
  • Be flexible on minor preferences to widen your options

Tour and Evaluate Listings Quickly

  • Schedule tours immediately after finding a promising listing
  • Check condition, appliances, plumbing, and neighborhood noise
  • Ask current tenants about utilities, maintenance, and landlord responsiveness
  • Bring a checklist to ensure you evaluate all aspects consistently

Use Timing to Your Advantage

  • March‚ÄìApril: still good options, but act quickly
  • May‚ÄìAugust: limited availability, consider subleases or less popular locations
üí° Tip: Have all application materials ready in advance (ID, student status, references, guarantor) so you can act fast when you find the right place.

Property Viewing Tips

Be prepared when touring properties so you can make informed decisions quickly.

What to Inspect

  • Appliances and plumbing: check stove, fridge, dishwasher, sink, shower, and toilet
  • Heating and cooling systems
  • Windows, doors, and locks for security
  • Signs of pests or water damage
  • Closets and storage space

Questions to Ask

  • How responsive is maintenance?
  • Who manages the property?
  • Are utilities included?
  • Parking availability and costs
  • Lease flexibility (renewals, early termination, subleasing)

Understanding Your Lease

Your lease is a legal contract. Understand it fully before signing.

Key Terms to Review

  • Lease duration and move-in/move-out dates
  • Rent amount, due dates, and late fees
  • Security deposit terms and conditions for return
  • Utilities and who is responsible
  • Maintenance responsibilities

Red Flags

  • Unclear or verbal-only agreements
  • Unusually high application fees
  • Restrictions not previously discussed
  • Pressure to sign immediately without review

Negotiation Tips

  • Ask for rent discounts or waived fees for early commitment
  • Negotiate move-in dates to match your schedule
  • Request maintenance improvements before signing
  • Document all agreed-upon changes in writing

Your Rights as a Tenant

Virginia tenants have rights. Know them to protect yourself and your security deposit.

Legal Protections

  • Landlords must provide habitable housing
  • Security deposits must be returned within 45 days after move-out
  • Tenants cannot be evicted without proper notice and legal process
  • Landlords must disclose all fees and policies in writing
  • Right to request repairs and reasonable accommodations

Resources
